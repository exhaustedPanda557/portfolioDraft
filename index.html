Sojourner House

Project's Context:

I selected a page of a website that had usability and accessibility issues and built a redesigned page to address the issues.

Sexual assault is a huge issue on college campuses and society at large. It is important to connect students to resources and raise awareness of the issue by highlighting organizations that provide assisstance to those in college and outside of college. Sojourner House is a Providence support center for those struggling with domestic violence, sexual assualt, and human trafficking.

Project's Process (click arrows on the slideshow for the progression):

First: I identified Useability issues

Second: I identified Accessibility issues

Third: I identified design modifications and correspondingly created a Hi-Fi mobile prototype

Fourth: I completed the same redesign process for a Hi-Fi tablet prototype

Fifth: I again completed the same redesign process for a Hi-Fi desktop prototype

Overall Takeaways

I improved accessbility with more high contrast colors and adding white bakcgrounds. To help students more quickly understand the importance and mission of the sexual assualt organization, I added a mission statement section that is viewed on the first screen. I moved to a more accessible section the user actions to "Get Help", "Support Us", and learn "About Us".

yHangry

*this was a group project*

Project's Context:

My group selected a yCombinator startup and designed an interactive interface for their service. We picked yHangry, a startup that connects customers with private chefs to cook private dinner parties at their homes. We created an online platforrm for yHangry users that allows them to peruse the meal options and book.

In college, it is easy to become overwhelmed by the number and intensity of alcoholic experiences. Our work to further develop a platform for yHangry is meant to support the importance of creating more intimate and healthy non-alcholic social experiences like dinner parties.

Project's Process (click arrows on the slideshow for the progression):

First: We identified what components the platform should include and what layout would be best. We created a wireframe for this.

Second: We created a hi-fi prototype and engaged in the critique process.

Third: We incorporated feedback from the critique

Fourth: We completed user testing, and from user's out-loud step-by-step comments, we realized the following takeaways.

Overall Takeaways

I learned how different webpages should flow into each other and connect through buttons. Communicating lots of information across multiple webpages requires simplicity and consistency in color scheme, fonts, icons, and the navigation bar.

Welcome to My Fridge

Project's Context:

I created an interactive list-based interace with React, composed of tying components to an internal data state. My interface allowed users to select food items from a list of what is in their refridgerator and aggreate the prep time for what is selected.

Food waste is a huge environmental and ethical issue, and it is especially difficult to address when you are a new cook. College can be the first time students buy groceries and cook for themselves. Unfortunately, the result can be forgotten food that has to be thrown out and unhealthy dinner choices. Thus, I created an interface to filter and sort based on calories, expiration status, food group category, and prep time. The interface allows users to create a healthy dinner recipe with low food waste and plan for the prep time (helpful for beginner college cooks).

Project's Process (click arrows on the slideshow for the progression):

First: I created a dinner item component to store data on the food.

Second: I created filtering and sorting to allow users to quickly identify what ingredients to use.

Third: I created a dinner recipe section that displays which items have been selected and aggregates their prep time.

Overall Takeaways

I created a simple and clear interface that identifies the three main user tasks: learn information about a food item, search for food items that satify low food waste and healthy criteria, and create a dinner recipe that helps beginner cooks by sharing the prep time needed.

Bartending Help

Project's Context:

I created mobile Flutter app using asynchronous calls to an outside data source of cocktail information to display bartending help.

College students do not know how to make sophisticated alcoholic drinks and settle for gatorade spiked with alcohol. The mobile app is convenient and makes it easy to click one button and learn a margarita receipe.

Project's Process (click arrows on the slideshow for the progression):

First: I identified a JSON with cocktail information that the mobile app can make asynchronous calls to.

Second: I created a button Widget that when users click it asynchronously gets Strawberry or Smash Watermelon Margarita drink instructions (respectively).

Overall Takeaways

It is most efficient to the user and creator if a buttons corresponds to just one piece of data from the JSON returned in an API call (i.e. each drink has its own button to get the bartending instructions).
